Enabling Broadcom Wireless
--------------------------

Broadcom BCM94352HMB is the best module that you can and should install in your laptop.

### Hardware Mod

First, you will need to mask certain PCB contacts with tiny pieces of kapton tape to prevent HP firmware from turning the Wi-Fi module off. Without this mod only Bluetooth part will work.

![BCM94352HMB Wi-Fi BIOS whitelist hardware hack](Resources/bcm94352hmb-hw-whitelist-hack.jpg)

Then, remove `IOath3kfrmwr.kext`, `Legacy/IOath3kfrmwr.kext`, `IOath3kdevice.kext`, `HS80211Family.kext`, `AirPortAtheros40.kext`, `ProBookAtheros.kext`, and `WifiLocFix.kext` entries from `config.plist`.

Finally, enable `AirportBrcmFixup.kext` (and its plugins), `BlueToolFixup.kext`, `BrcmBluetoothInjector.kext`, `BrcmFirmwareData.kext`, and `BrcmPatchRAM3.kext`. Copy and paste the following section where Atheros configuration was previously:

Mojave and below: Copy `BrcmPatchRAM2.kext` and `BrcmFirmwareRepo.kext` (not “Data”) to `/Library/Extensions`, and rebuild kernel cache.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> These two kexts cannot be injected from bootloader and must be installed manually.

If you don‘t get Wi-Fi it means your card needs a [firmware uploader](https://github.com/acidanthera/BrcmPatchRAM#brcmbluetoothinjectorkext), so switch them around. Add Broadcom configuration parameters to OpenCore `config.plist` `boot-args` with your country code (`NVRAM/Add/7C436110-AB2A-4BBB-A880-FE41995C9F82`):

```xml
        <key>boot-args</key>
        <string>-no_compat_check amfi_get_out_of_my_way=1 amfi=0x80 brcmfx-driver=1 brcmfx-country=US</string>
```

### Disable 30s Series Wi-Fi Whitelist

**For ProBook 4x30s series only:** enable Wi-Fi BIOS whitelist bypass EFI driver in `UEFI/Drivers` (`Enabled` -> `true`):

```xml
      <dict>
        <key>Arguments</key>
        <string></string>
        <key>Comment</key>
        <string>ProBookWifiWhlistOff.efi</string>
        <key>Enabled</key>
        <true/>
        <key>LoadEarly</key>
        <false/>
        <key>Path</key>
        <string>ProBookWifiUnblock.efi</string>
      </dict>
```

**Do not use this driver with any other laptop: doing so can brick your device!**

The laptop firmware will still warn you about incompatible wireless card installed (just skip it with <kbd>Enter</kbd>), but it would no longer be actually disabled, despite what the warning says.
